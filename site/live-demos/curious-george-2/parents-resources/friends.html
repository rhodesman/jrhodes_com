Friends of George

  • Gossie & Friends

    Gossie and her friends are small goslings with big personalities!  The gaggle of goslings tromp merrily through the farmyard…playing, sharing, and learning about friendship. Gossie & Friends books are perfectly attuned to the interests and concerns of preschoolers.

    Visit the Gossie & Friends Site

  • Five Little Monkeys

    Children will enjoy the silly antics of these little monkeys while learning about addition and subtraction. These zany monkeys teach children about sibling teamwork and loving family dynamics.

    Energetic illustrations and rhyming text highlight the playfulness and ingenuity of these little monkeys.

    Visit the Five Little Monkeys Site

  • Tacky the Penguin

    From his signature Hawaiian shirt to his witty play on words, Tacky is merrily, confidently not your average bird. Whether he’s entertaining the Emperor penguin on short notice or sending some penguin hunters packing across the Nice Icy Land,Tacky always finds a successful and imaginative resolution.

    From his tidy and precise companions to the exotic animals he meets on his journeys, Tacky embraces everyone.

    Books featuring Tacky the Penguin

  • George and Martha

    Like all best friends, George and Martha do everything together—go to the movies, play at the beach, and just hang around not doing much of anything. George and Martha teach each other (and adoring readers) that even in a close friendship, privacy is important, practical jokes can sometimes backfire, and among other things, pouring split pea soup into your loafers to spare the chef’s feelings is not the best-laid plan. 

    Books featuring George and Martha

  • Lyle, Lyle, Crocodile

    With a SWISH, SWASH, SPLASH, and SWOOSH! Lyle came into the lives of the Primm family and the world of children’s books. Over the years, Lyle has done all sorts of things including saving his neighbors from a fire, entertaining hospital patients, and going to the jungle to find his mother. He likes to skip double-dutch, watch building construction, ice skate, and eat his favorite food, Turkish caviar. The adventures of Lyle the crocodile have entertained children for more than forty years.

    Books featuring Lyle, Lyle, Crocodile

  • Mike Mulligan

    Since it was first published in 1939, Mike Mulligan and His Steam Shovel has delighted generations of children. Mike and his trusty steam shovel, Mary Anne, have a very important job. They dig deep canals for boats to travel through, cut mountain passes for trains, and hollow out cellars for big city skyscrapers — the very symbol of modern industrial America. But with progress comes new machines, and soon the inseparable duo are out of work. Mike believes that Mary Anne can dig as much in a day as a hundred men can dig in a week, and the two have one last chance to prove it and save Mary Anne from the scrap heap. What happens next in the small town of Popperville is a testament to their friendship, as well as old-fashioned hard work and ingenuity.

    Books featuring Mike Mulligan
