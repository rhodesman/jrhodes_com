Still Curious about Halloween?

Halloween is a great opportunity for encouraging children's imagination and creativity, as they think about and plan the costume they want to wear. After Halloween ends, those piles of candy are perfect for fun activities that help develop children's early math skills, such as sorting and counting. For example:

  • Encourage your children to sort their candy into piles based on different features, such as like/dislike, chocolate/not chocolate, big/small. Which pile has the most? Which pile has the least?
  • Help children count the number of candies in each pile.
  • How else can they sort their candy?
